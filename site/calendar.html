How to Use the Economic Calendar

The economic calendar is a structural planning tool for identifying upcoming macroeconomic events that may introduce volatility or directional bias into the instruments covered by ALTIVOR frameworks. Use it to prepare, not to react.

Browse or Search

Scroll through the week's events by day, or use the search bar to locate specific releases such as "Non-Farm Payrolls" or "ECB". The calendar updates each week with the latest scheduled data.

Filter by Impact

High-impact events such as interest rate decisions, NFP, and CPI releases carry the greatest potential for market disruption. Filter to high-impact to focus on these structural events during pre-session analysis.

Plan Your Week

Review the calendar before each trading week to identify sessions with clustered high-impact releases. These sessions may require adjusted position sizing or avoidance depending on your framework rules.

Country and Category Filters

Isolate events by country or category to focus on the economies and data types most relevant to your active instrument. For US100 participants, filtering to US events provides the most operationally relevant view.

Time Zone Awareness

All event times are displayed in CET (Central European Time). Convert to your local time zone before session planning. High-impact releases often cluster around US market open (15:30 CET) and European open (09:00 CET).

Volatility Management

Consider reducing position size or avoiding new entries 15-30 minutes before high-impact releases. Unexpected data can cause rapid price movements that may trigger stops or invalidate technical setups.
