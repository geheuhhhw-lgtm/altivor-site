01Getting Started

ALTIVOR INSTITUTE is an operational framework provider focused on structured, rules-based trading discipline. We provide behavioral and procedural validation systems not investment advice, signals, or managed execution services.

Our core product is the US100 Process Validation Challenge, a 2-month discipline framework for NASDAQ-100 Index CFD trading.

To begin, you must first complete the PREPARE Qualification Stage (29 ). This is a mandatory procedural gate that validates your ability to execute a declared trading process without deviation.

After completing the required compliance threshold in PREPARE, you gain access to purchase the US100 Challenge.

Our frameworks are designed for participants who already trade live or simulated accounts with regularity and possess understanding of market structure, price action, and risk management fundamentals.

If you are a complete beginner, we recommend building foundational knowledge before enrolling. ALTIVOR evaluates behavioral execution, not trading education.

The platform supports three visual themes Dark, Light, and Navy switchable via the top bar controls.

Multiple languages are available including English, Polish, German, French, Spanish, Hindi, and Russian.

02US100 Challenge

It evaluates process adherence not financial performance. A participant who follows all rules and incurs a loss has performed at a higher standard than one who violates rules and generates profit.

03PREPARE Qualification

PREPARE is a procedural gate, not a performance benchmark. Its sole objective is to validate your ability to execute a declared execution process under funded-style conditions without deviation.

You must complete the required compliance threshold to qualify. Each trade is classified strictly as PASS or FAIL based on rule compliance financial outcome has no evaluative value.

If you reach the disqualification threshold, PREPARE access is temporarily suspended with a mandatory cooldown period.

This mechanism exists to prevent reactive execution patterns following consecutive procedural failures. Access is automatically restored after the cooldown expires.

Each trade submission requires MT5 History Data including:

  • Symbol, Ticket ID
  • Open Time and Close Time (CET)
  • Entry Price, Stop Loss, Volume (lots)
  • Profit/Loss (USD)
  • A mandatory screenshot of your MT5 account history

You must confirm the data matches your MT5 Account History export exactly.

06Risk, Documentation & Process

The framework specifies a fixed fractional risk allocation per trade as the default parameter. This is defined at cycle commencement and is non-modifiable during the active period. Complete operational parameters are documented in the Framework Pack.

Participants declare their risk allocation as part of their process documentation. Deviation from the declared risk constitutes a rule violation.

The ledger is a factual record of execution not a diary or narrative. It provides an auditable trail of entry, exit, risk, and outcome for each trade.

Failure to maintain the ledger according to the defined structure constitutes an automatic challenge invalidation. The journal component is optional and must never be used to justify ledger results.

The framework enforces zero re-entries after missed trades. If you miss a compliant setup, it remains missed. Chasing price or taking a late entry after a missed opportunity is not permitted.

This rule exists to prevent reactive, emotionally-driven entries that deviate from the declared process.

09Behavioral Discipline & Process Control

A revenge trade is any execution taken in direct reaction to a previous loss typically with increased size, weakened criteria, or outside the declared plan. The framework classifies this as a CRITICAL behavioral violation.

The Execution Checklist includes no-trade filters specifically designed to detect revenge-trading patterns before entry. Repeated revenge trades trigger escalating override warnings.

Soft violations reduce your compliance score but do not automatically fail the session examples include suboptimal entry timing or incomplete journal notes.

Critical violations such as missing Stop Loss, exceeding risk limits, or trading outside session hours result in immediate trade failure regardless of financial outcome.

When the Execution Checklist determines a trade should not be taken, the override system presents escalating warnings. The first override shows a base warning. At 2+ session violations, a pattern-recognition layer appears. At 3+ violations, a red-state layer indicates potential loss of control.

You may still proceed, but every override decision is audited. The system is designed to create friction against impulsive execution, not to prevent trading entirely.

Trade Alignment is a context quality score generated by the Execution Checklist. It evaluates market type, volatility, session conditions, ADR usage, selected liquidity anchors, and directional bias to produce a percentage from 0100.

This score explains how well current conditions match your declared process. It does not override hard blockers those are binary PASS/FAIL.

The framework identifies the Post-Win Day as a hidden risk scenario. After a strong positive result, the correct behavior is to maintain or reduce exposure not increase it.

There is no hard restriction, but the behavioral model flags post-win overconfidence patterns. If your declared process does not account for post-win behavior, additional trades may be flagged.

No-trade filters are hard blockers in the Execution Checklist that prevent trade execution when structural conditions are not met FOMC days, first 15 minutes of session, incomplete ADR levels, or active emotional state indicators.

The framework validates correct inaction as a legitimate behavioral outcome. Not trading when conditions are wrong is as important as trading when they are right.

Overtrading is executing more trades than your declared process authorizes in a given session. The framework tracks same-day trade count against your stated maximum frequency.

Exceeding your declared daily limit even with compliant trades constitutes a behavioral violation. The system treats frequency discipline as a structural rule.

The discipline streak counts consecutive compliant trades. It measures behavioral consistency over time not profit. Breaking the streak through a violation resets the counter.

A long streak demonstrates sustained adherence to your declared process. It is a behavioral indicator, not a performance metric.

No. Zero-trade days are valid. The framework does not require daily execution. If your declared process has no qualifying setup on a given day, not trading is the correct decision.

Forced trading to maintain activity is a behavioral red flag, not a positive signal.

11Funded Accounts & Preparation

A funded account is a trading account where capital is provided by a third-party firm — commonly called a proprietary trading firm or "prop firm" — rather than the trader's own capital. Access typically requires passing an evaluation that tests execution discipline, risk control, and behavioral consistency under defined parameters.

The structural constraints used in these evaluations — fixed risk, drawdown limits, session rules, documentation requirements — closely mirror the parameters built into the ALTIVOR framework. ALTIVOR is designed to develop and validate execution discipline under exactly these kinds of conditions.

The ALTIVOR framework — PREPARE, the US100 Challenge, and the Execution Checklist — is built around the same structural constraints typically imposed in funded account evaluations: fixed risk per trade, maximum daily exposure, session boundaries, behavioral consistency requirements, and full documentation standards.

Participation develops execution discipline under funded-style conditions. ALTIVOR does not guarantee, imply, or promise access to any funded account as a result of participation.
