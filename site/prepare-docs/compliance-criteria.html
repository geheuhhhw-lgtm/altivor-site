PREPARE Document 03

Compliance Evaluation Criteria

Detailed evaluation standards for each trade submission, including pass/fail conditions, violation examples, and edge case clarifications.

Section 01

Evaluation Overview

Each trade submission undergoes evaluation against six structural criteria. The evaluation is binary — a trade either passes all criteria (COMPLIANT) or fails one or more criteria (NON-COMPLIANT).

There is no weighted scoring. A trade that fails a single criterion receives the same classification as a trade that fails all criteria. The system measures procedural adherence, not degree of compliance.

Evaluation Principle: The question is not "how well did you follow the process?" but "did you follow the process completely?" Partial compliance is non-compliance.

Section 02

Individual Criteria

1
Position Completion

Every submitted trade must be a fully closed position with both entry and exit recorded. Open positions, partial closes, or positions still in progress cannot be submitted.

PASS

Trade opened at 14:32, closed at 15:47. Both timestamps recorded with entry and exit prices.

FAIL

Trade opened at 14:32, still running. Exit time and price left blank or marked as "pending."

2
Stop Loss Presence

A stop loss level must be defined and documented for every trade. The stop loss must represent a genuine protective level, not an arbitrary value entered to satisfy the requirement.

PASS

Stop loss set at 18,450 for a long position entered at 18,500. Level visible on chart screenshot.

FAIL

Stop loss field left empty, or set to 0, or set to a level that would never realistically be hit.

3
Risk Allocation

Risk per trade must not exceed 0.25% of the declared account balance. This is calculated as: (Entry Price - Stop Loss) × Position Size ÷ Account Balance × 100.

PASS

$10,000 account, 50-point stop, 0.05 lots. Risk = $25 = 0.25% of account.

FAIL

$10,000 account, 50-point stop, 0.15 lots. Risk = $75 = 0.75% of account. Exceeds 0.25% limit.

4
Screenshot Evidence

A TradingView chart screenshot must accompany each submission. The screenshot should show the trade context, entry/exit levels, and stop loss placement.

PASS

Clear TradingView screenshot showing price action, entry marker, and stop loss level annotation.

FAIL

No screenshot attached, or screenshot from different platform, or image too blurry to verify details.

5
Data Accuracy

All submitted data must match broker records exactly. Entry price, exit price, position size, and timestamps must be accurate to the values recorded by your broker.

PASS

Submitted entry 18,502.5 matches broker statement showing 18,502.5 fill price.

FAIL

Submitted entry 18,500 but broker shows 18,502.5. Discrepancy indicates data manipulation or error.

6
Field Completeness

All required form fields must be populated. Empty fields, placeholder values, or obviously invalid entries result in automatic non-compliance.

PASS

All fields completed: instrument, direction, entry, exit, stop loss, size, account balance, timestamps.

FAIL

Account balance field left empty. System cannot calculate risk percentage without this value.

Section 03

Edge Cases

The following clarifications address common edge case questions:

  • Breakeven stops: A stop loss moved to breakeven after entry is acceptable. The original stop loss must still be documented.
  • Trailing stops: Trailing stops are acceptable. Document the initial stop loss level at entry.
  • Multiple positions: Each position must be submitted separately. Averaging into a position creates multiple trades.
  • Partial closes: Submit only when the entire position is closed. Partial close submissions are non-compliant.
  • Demo accounts: Demo account trades are acceptable. The same criteria apply regardless of account type.
Section 04

Outcome Independence

Financial outcome has zero weight in compliance evaluation. The following scenarios illustrate this principle:

Compliant Loss: Trade loses $25 but follows all structural requirements. Stop loss was hit. Risk was within limits. Screenshot provided. This trade is COMPLIANT.

Non-Compliant Win: Trade profits $150 but was entered without a stop loss. The trader "got lucky" and price moved favorably. This trade is NON-COMPLIANT.

The system evaluates process, not results. A participant who consistently follows the process but experiences losses is demonstrating the discipline PREPARE is designed to validate. A participant who profits while breaking rules is demonstrating exactly the behavior PREPARE is designed to filter out.
