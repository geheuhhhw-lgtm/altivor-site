Trade Submission Guidelines

Complete guide to the trade submission process, including required fields, screenshot specifications, and common errors to avoid.

Section 01

Required Fields

Every trade submission requires the following fields to be completed accurately. Missing or invalid data in any required field will result in automatic non-compliance.

Field Format Notes
* Instrument Symbol (e.g., US100, EURUSD) Must match broker symbol exactly
* Direction LONG or SHORT Select from dropdown
* Entry Price Decimal number Exact fill price from broker
* Exit Price Decimal number Exact close price from broker
* Stop Loss Decimal number Initial stop loss level at entry
* Position Size Lots (e.g., 0.05) Standard lot notation
* Account Balance Currency amount Balance at time of trade entry
* Entry Time Date + Time When position was opened
* Exit Time Date + Time When position was closed
* Screenshot Image file TradingView chart required
Section 02

Screenshot Requirements

Screenshots serve as visual verification of your trade setup. They must meet the following specifications:

Source Platform

  • Screenshots must be from TradingView
  • Other charting platforms are not accepted
  • Broker platform screenshots are not sufficient

Required Content

  • Price action context around the trade
  • Entry level clearly visible or marked
  • Stop loss level clearly visible or marked
  • Instrument symbol visible in chart header

Quality Standards

  • Image must be clear and readable
  • Minimum resolution: 800x600 pixels
  • Accepted formats: PNG, JPG, JPEG
  • Maximum file size: 5MB

Important: Blurry, cropped, or manipulated screenshots will result in non-compliance. The screenshot must accurately represent the trade as executed.

Section 03

Submission Process

Follow these steps to submit a trade for qualification:

  1. Wait for your trade to fully close. Open positions cannot be submitted.
  2. Capture a TradingView screenshot showing your trade setup.
  3. Navigate to the PREPARE qualification system.
  4. Fill in all required fields with accurate data from your broker.
  5. Upload your TradingView screenshot.
  6. Review all information for accuracy.
  7. Click Submit to send your trade for evaluation.

Finality: Once submitted, a trade cannot be modified, withdrawn, or resubmitted. The compliance determination is immediate and final. Double-check all data before submitting.

Section 04

Common Errors

The following errors frequently cause non-compliance. Review this list before each submission:

Missing Stop Loss

Stop loss field left empty or set to 0. Every trade must have a defined stop loss level.

Risk Exceeds 0.25%

Position size too large relative to stop loss distance and account balance. Recalculate before trading.

Wrong Screenshot Platform

Screenshot from MT4/MT5 or broker platform instead of TradingView. Only TradingView screenshots are accepted.

Price Discrepancy

Submitted entry/exit prices don't match broker records. Always copy exact values from your broker statement.

Open Position Submission

Attempting to submit a trade that hasn't closed yet. Wait for full position closure before submitting.

Empty Account Balance

Account balance field not filled. This field is required to calculate risk percentage.

Section 05

Best Practices

Follow these recommendations to maximize your compliance rate:

  • Prepare screenshots in advance: Capture your TradingView chart before closing the trade while the setup is still visible.
  • Use a checklist: Before each trade, verify that your position size results in ≤0.25% risk.
  • Submit promptly: Submit trades soon after closing while details are fresh and broker data is easily accessible.
  • Double-check numbers: Copy entry/exit prices directly from your broker to avoid transcription errors.
  • Review before submit: Use the preview feature to verify all data before final submission.

Mindset: Treat each submission as a formal record. The discipline required to submit accurate, complete trade data is the same discipline required for consistent trading execution.
