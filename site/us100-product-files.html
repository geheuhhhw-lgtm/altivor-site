US100 Process Validation Challenge

Case Study Process-Only Execution

Instrument US100 (NASDAQ futures)
Session Type NY Continuation Window
Market Condition Directionally resolved session (trend continuation)
Evaluation PASS process only

Document intent. This file is presented as a case-study product entry. It illustrates process execution under real market conditions and should be read as an operational example, not as a performance report, strategy tutorial or repeatability claim.

Purpose Context Pre-trade Conditions Execution Phase Stress and Uncertainty Trade Resolution Post-trade Procedure Evaluation Key Takeaway

Purpose of this case study

This case study exists to illustrate process execution under real market conditions.

It is not a performance report. It is not a strategy tutorial. It does not imply repeatability of outcomes.

The objective is to show how discipline, not prediction, governs decision-making during a valid trade.

Context

  • Instrument: US100 (NASDAQ futures)
  • Session type: NY Continuation Window
  • Market condition: Directionally resolved session (trend continuation)

By the time this trade was executed, the market had already established a clear directional bias. The case does not focus on identifying the bias. It focuses on acting correctly once the bias was accepted.

Pre-trade conditions

Before execution, the following conditions were satisfied:

  • Trading occurred strictly within the allowed time window.
  • Market structure had already resolved earlier in the session.
  • No trades were taken earlier that day.
  • No pressure existed to force activity.
  • At this stage, not trading was still a valid outcome. The trade was taken only after a compliant opportunity appeared.

Execution phase

Once the setup aligned with the participants predefined criteria:

  • Entry was executed without anticipation.
  • Position size respected fixed risk limits.
  • Stop Loss was placed based on structure and volatility, not proximity.

At no point was the trade managed based on unrealized P&L. All decisions were made relative to market structure and predefined rules.

Stress and uncertainty

During the trade:

  • Price moved into an area close to Stop Loss.
  • No protective adjustments were made.
  • No early exit was taken to reduce emotional discomfort.

This phase represents the primary psychological test.

The correct action was inaction.

Trade resolution

As the session progressed:

  • Market structure remained intact.
  • Volatility expanded in the direction of the established bias.
  • The position was held without rule modification.
  • The trade was closed only after predefined exit conditions were met.

The outcome was positive.

However, the result is irrelevant to the evaluation.

Post-trade procedure

After closure:

  • The trade was recorded in the official ledger.
  • Only objective data were logged (RR, USD, duration).
  • No narrative justification was added.
  • No additional trades were taken during the session.
PASS

This trade is classified as PASS not because it was profitable, but because:

  • All rules were respected.
  • No discretionary deviations occurred.
  • Emotional impulses did not influence execution.
  • A losing outcome under the same behavior would have resulted in the same evaluation.

Key takeaway

This case demonstrates a core principle of the Challenge:

Correct behavior does not require confidence. It requires restraint.

The edge emerges not from frequent activity, but from the ability to remain inactive until conditions justify participation.

File 02 Regulatory Document Released Product Entry

US100 Process Validation Challenge

Regulatory & Evaluation Document

Document Role Binding Governing Layer
Stage Structure PREPARE CHALLENGE
Evaluation Basis PASS / FAIL only
Authority Prevails over operational documents

Document role. This file constitutes the detailed governing layer of the US100 Challenge. It should be read as the binding regulatory and evaluation document for the programme, while operational guides and templates remain subordinate.

1. Purpose & Hierarchy 2. Entry Conditions 3. Stages & Activation 4. Evaluation Scope 5. Operational Constraints 6. Documentation 7. Failure Logic 8. Strategy Independence 9. Final Statement

1. Purpose, Intent and Framework Hierarchy

1.1 Statement of Intent and Philosophical Position

The US100 Process Validation Challenge is intentionally structured as a selective, exclusionary, and demanding regulatory framework.

Its purpose is not to maximize participation, completion rates, or engagement, but to identify and validate participants capable of sustained procedural discipline under real market conditions.

This framework does not exist to teach trading, optimize strategies, enhance performance, or validate market skill.

It exists solely to evaluate whether a participant can execute a declared execution process under funded-style conditions without deviation, regardless of financial outcome, emotional pressure, or subjective confidence.

Difficulty, attrition, and disqualification are expected and integral outcomes of the Challenge.

They do not represent failure of the framework, but confirmation of its intended function.

Participation is appropriate only for individuals who explicitly accept that process integrity is valued above results, effort, intent, or profitability.

1.2 Document Role and Hierarchy

It defines:

  • stage structure and activation logic,
  • evaluation rules (PASS / FAIL),
  • operational constraints (risk limits and trading windows),
  • participant responsibility.

This document intentionally does not duplicate operational procedures.

Operational documents (subordinate to this framework):

  • Participant Onboarding Guide how to operate day-to-day and what to expect
  • Execution & Trading Guide execution mechanics and trade lifecycle rules
  • Ledger & Journal Template mandatory documentation formats
  • Case Study (Process-Only) illustrative examples of compliant behavior

In case of conflict, this Regulatory & Evaluation Document prevails.

2. Entry Conditions and Baseline

2.1 Entry Condition

Participation is permitted only after completion of:

  • Six (6) fully compliant trades, executed strictly according to a declared execution process under funded-style conditions.

A trade is considered compliant only if it meets the PASS criteria defined in Section 3 and the fail logic of Section 7.

2.2 PREPARE Stage as Procedural Gate

The PREPARE stage serves as a procedural gate, not a performance benchmark.

Results achieved during PREPARE:

  • do not constitute performance benchmarks,
  • must not be interpreted as expected outcomes during the Challenge,
  • have no evaluative weight beyond procedural compliance.

Evaluation during PREPARE is based exclusively on:

  • rule adherence,
  • execution discipline,
  • process consistency.

3. Challenge Stages and Activation Rules

3.1 Overview

The Challenge operates as a two-stage evaluation framework whose exclusive purpose is to assess behavioral and procedural consistency, not trading performance.

Stages:

  • PREPARE Stage procedural validation phase
  • CHALLENGE Structured execution under funded constraints. Fixed risk. Measurable validation.

Progression is strictly sequential. No stage may be skipped.

3.2 PREPARE Stage Purpose

The PREPARE stage functions as a procedural gateway.

Its sole objective is to verify that the participant is capable of:

  • executing trades strictly according to a predefined and declared process,
  • respecting predefined risk constraints without exception,
  • maintaining discipline under live market conditions,
  • operating without emotional, impulsive, or anticipatory behavior.

The PREPARE stage is not intended to:

  • optimize strategies,
  • evaluate market skill or predictive accuracy,
  • generate profit,
  • validate edge, expectancy, or adaptability.

Any interpretation of PREPARE as a performance phase is invalid.

3.3 PREPARE Stage PASS / FAIL Evaluation

Evaluation during PREPARE is exclusively process-based.

Each trade is classified strictly as:

  • PASS
  • FAIL

Classification is determined solely by rule compliance, regardless of financial outcome.

Normative execution mechanics (e.g., timeframes, triggers, stop methodology, position management) are defined in the Execution & Trading Guide and are operationally binding, but subordinate to this framework.

3.4 What PREPARE Does Not Measure

The following elements are explicitly excluded from evaluation:

  • profitability or loss,
  • win rate / strike rate,
  • risk-to-reward ratios,
  • trade frequency,
  • strategy effectiveness,
  • instrument selection.

Positive or negative financial outcomes achieved during PREPARE carry no evaluative value.

3.5 Transition to CHALLENGE Stage

Completion of PREPARE does not automatically activate the Challenge.

Transition occurs only after:

  • completion of the required number of compliant (PASS) trades,
  • verification that all procedural criteria were met without exception.

The document itself does not track participant status.

3.6 Participant Responsibility

Participants retain full and exclusive responsibility for:

  • strategy design,
  • instrument selection,
  • execution decisions.

The Challenge evaluates behavioral execution of a declared process, not market insight, prediction skill, or trading performance.

4. Purpose and Scope of Evaluation

4.1 Primary Objective

The primary objective is to validate the:

  • robustness,
  • repeatability,
  • psychological sustainability

of the participant's declared execution process under funded-style conditions in live market environments.

The Challenge assesses whether the process can be:

  • executed consistently over time,
  • repeated without degradation of discipline,
  • sustained psychologically without rule erosion or emotional escalation.

The objective is process validation, not performance optimization.

4.2 Performance Context

The following performance ranges may be used to contextualize the process under realistic market conditions across the Challenge window:

  • Target cumulative return across the two-month Challenge window: approximately 6%
  • Acceptable minimum average return: approximately 3% per month

These ranges:

  • are not passing criteria,
  • do not override rule compliance,
  • cannot validate non-compliant execution.

Any outcome achieved through procedural breach is treated as procedurally void.

4.3 Interpretation Responsibility

Responsibility for understanding the structure, intent, and consequences of this framework rests entirely with the participant.

Misinterpretation of the Challenge as a performance evaluation, educational program, or profit-oriented assessment does not alter nor modify its regulatory meaning.

Participation constitutes explicit acknowledgment that this framework evaluates behavioral execution only, not competence, intelligence, experience, or potential.

5. Operational Constraints

5.1 Reference Instrument and Capital

Reference Instrument: US100 (NASDAQ Futures)

Reference Capital: 100,000 USD

All risk calculations and performance references are derived from the reference capital baseline.

5.2 Risk Parameters

Fixed risk governance aligned with funded requirements within the Challenge is non-negotiable:

  • Fixed risk per trade: 0.25% of reference capital (1R)
  • Maximum number of trades per day: 3

Risk per trade must remain constant regardless of:

  • confidence level,
  • perceived setup quality,
  • recent performance (wins or losses),
  • market conditions.

Any deviation constitutes a procedural breach.

5.3 Trading Windows

Trading decisions are permitted exclusively within:

  • NY Core Session: 15:45 17:30
  • NY Continuation Session: 19:30 20:30

Outside these windows:

NO TRADE is the default, correct, and expected decision.

Positions opened within permitted windows may be managed beyond them, provided that:

  • no new exposure is added outside the windows, and
  • management remains consistent with the declared process.

6. Documentation Requirements

All executed trades must be recorded in the official ledger.

The ledger serves as the objective process record.

Documentation formats, fields, and rules are defined in the Ledger & Journal Template and are mandatory.

A trade not recorded in the ledger is treated as non-existent for evaluation purposes.

7. Failure Logic

7.1 Binary Evaluation Framework

The Challenge is evaluated on a binary basis:

  • PASS full compliance
  • FAIL any procedural breach

Profitability does not override failure.

Material breaches include, but are not limited to:

  • violation of trading windows,
  • violation of fixed risk limits,
  • discretionary rule modification during execution,
  • failure to document trades according to ledger requirements.

Detailed disqualification triggers are defined in the Fail Conditions section of the operational documentation, which is subordinate to this framework.

7.2 Finality and Non-Appealability of Evaluation

All PASS / FAIL determinations issued under this framework are final and non-appealable.

No contextual explanations, subjective interpretations, post-hoc justifications, or external factors including market conditions or financial outcomes may be invoked to override, reinterpret, or reassess a failure classification.

Acceptance of this finality and non-appealability constitutes a mandatory condition of participation in the Challenge.

8. Strategy Independence Clause

The Challenge does not require participants to replicate any specific trading strategy, setup, or methodology.

Participants may trade any strategy provided that:

  • it is declared prior to execution,
  • includes objective entry and invalidation rules,
  • respects the fixed risk constraints of this framework,
  • allows objective evaluation through the ledger.

Strategy selection is not evaluated. Execution discipline is.

Binding

9. Final Statement

This document constitutes the binding regulatory framework for the full duration of the US100 Process Validation Challenge.

All guides, templates, and case studies are operational supplements and remain subordinate to this framework.

Operational Parameters & Execution Specifications

Document Type Operational Specification
Access Level Framework Pack & US100 Challenge
Authority Binding Execution Layer
Version 1.0 Active

Document intent. This file contains the exact operational parameters required for challenge execution. These specifications are binding and must be followed precisely. Deviation from any parameter constitutes a process violation.

Risk Parameters Session Windows Position Sizing Trade Limits Entry Protocol Exit Protocol Compliance Scoring

Risk Parameters

Fixed Risk Per Trade: 0.25% of account equity

Maximum Daily Risk Exposure: 0.75% (3 trades 0.25%)

Risk Tolerance: 5% deviation from reference risk value

The first compliant trade establishes the reference risk value. All subsequent trades must match this value within the tolerance band. Trades outside this band are flagged as non-compliant.

Session Windows

Permitted Trading Window: 15:30 21:00 CET (New York Session)

Trade Opening Requirement: Position must be opened within the permitted window

Trade Closing: No restriction positions may be held beyond 21:00 CET

Trades opened outside the permitted window are automatically flagged as non-compliant, regardless of outcome.

Position Sizing Formula

Lot Size Calculation:

Lot Size = (Account Equity 0.0025) (Stop Loss in Points Point Value)

Example:

  • Account Equity: 10,000
  • Risk Amount: 25 (0.25%)
  • Stop Loss: 50 points
  • Point Value: 1 per point per lot
  • Lot Size: 0.50 lots

Position size must be calculated before entry. Adjusting position size after entry is prohibited.

Trade Limits

Maximum Trades Per Day: 3

Minimum Holding Time: 1 minute

Maximum Non-Compliant Trades: 20 (triggers 7-day cooldown)

Qualification Threshold: 10 compliant trades

Days with zero trades are considered compliant if no rule violations occurred. There is no obligation to trade every session.

Entry Protocol

Before opening any position, verify:

  1. Time Check: Current time is within 15:3021:00 CET
  2. Daily Limit: Fewer than 3 trades executed today
  3. Risk Calculation: Position size calculated at 0.25% risk
  4. Stop Loss: Predetermined stop level identified
  5. Instrument: US100 or approved equivalent symbol

Entry without completing all verification steps constitutes a process violation.

Exit Protocol

Stop Loss: Must be placed immediately upon entry. Moving stop loss to increase risk is prohibited.

Take Profit: Optional. May be set at entry or managed manually.

Trailing Stop: Permitted only in the direction of profit protection.

Manual Exit: Permitted at any time. Early exits are not penalized.

Removing or widening stop loss after entry results in automatic non-compliance flag.

Compliance Scoring

Each trade is evaluated against 7 compliance rules:

  1. Approved instrument (US100 or equivalent)
  2. Trade opened within permitted window
  3. Stop loss present and valid
  4. Risk within fixed allocation (5%)
  5. Daily trade limit respected
  6. No duplicate ticket submission
  7. Minimum holding time met

Compliance Threshold: 80% (minimum 6 of 7 rules)

Trades meeting ?80% of rules are marked COMPLIANT. Trades below 80% are marked NON-COMPLIANT.

Operational

Binding Notice

These parameters are non-negotiable. Deviation from any specification regardless of intent or outcome constitutes a process violation.

The framework evaluates execution, not results. A losing trade executed correctly is superior to a winning trade executed incorrectly.

Fail Conditions & Rules of Disqualification

Document Role Binding Disqualification Layer
Evaluation Mode Binary PASS / FAIL
Trigger Standard Single Violation
Authority Outcome cannot override breach

Document intent. This file defines the explicit conditions under which the US100 Process Validation Challenge is considered failed. It exists to remove ambiguity, prevent self-justification, and ensure that evaluation remains governed by process integrity rather than outcome.

Purpose Fundamental Rule Automatic Challenge Failure 1. Time Windows 2. Stop Loss Manipulation 3. Re-entry After Missed Setup 4. Criteria Modification 5. Emotional Trading 6. Overtrading 7. Ledger Contamination No Exceptions Clause Final Statement

Purpose of this document

This document defines explicit conditions under which the Challenge is considered failed.

It exists to remove ambiguity. It exists to prevent self-justification. It exists to ensure that results are evaluated by process integrity, not outcomes.

If a situation is listed below, no explanation, profit, or intention can override it.

Binary

Fundamental rule

The Challenge is binary.

You either respect the rules or you fail.

There is no partial credit. There are no exceptions. A single violation is sufficient to invalidate the Challenge.

Automatic Challenge Failure

The Challenge is immediately failed if any of the following occur:

1. Trading outside allowed time windows

Definition: Executing a trade outside the predefined NY Core or NY Continuation windows.

Includes:

  • early entries before the window opens,
  • late entries after the window closes,
  • "management entries" justified as exceptions.

Rationale: Time discipline is part of the edge. Ignoring it invalidates all results.

2. Stop Loss manipulation

Definition: Any modification of Stop Loss not derived from structure and ATR.

Includes:

  • tightening SL to reduce discomfort,
  • widening SL to avoid being stopped,
  • moving SL based on P&L, not structure.

Rationale: Stop Loss exists to protect the process. Emotional SL adjustments corrupt all statistics.

3. Re-entry after a missed setup

Definition: Entering a trade after the original valid entry opportunity has passed.

Includes:

  • late entries due to hesitation,
  • chasing continuation candles,
  • "second chance" executions.

Rationale: Missed setups are closed opportunities. Re-entries convert discipline into impulse.

4. Criteria modification during execution

Definition: Changing entry, exit, or management rules once a trade is active.

Includes:

  • redefining BOS or structure mid-trade,
  • altering RR targets to fit price action,
  • post-hoc justification of decisions.

Rationale: Rules must exist before the trade. Changing them during execution invalidates the test.

5. Trading for emotional reasons

Definition: Executing trades driven by psychological state rather than setup validity.

Includes:

  • revenge trading after a loss,
  • confidence trading after a win,
  • boredom trading during slow sessions,
  • "not wanting to waste the day" trades.

Rationale: The Challenge tests emotional neutrality. Emotion-driven trades defeat its purpose.

6. Overtrading

Definition: Exceeding the maximum number of trades per day.

Includes:

  • splitting one idea into multiple entries,
  • attempting to recover earlier mistakes.

Rationale: Quantity does not compensate for poor discipline.

7. Ledger contamination

Definition: Altering, omitting, or decorating ledger entries.

Includes:

  • missing trades in the ledger,
  • adding narrative explanations,
  • modifying historical entries.

Rationale: The ledger is a factual record. Manipulating it invalidates evaluation.

No exceptions clause

Profit does not excuse violations. Loss does not justify rule bending. Intentions do not override behavior.

The Challenge evaluates what was done, not what was meant.

Finality

Final statement

This Challenge is not failed by losses. It is failed by loss of discipline.

If you complete the full duration without triggering any Fail Conditions regardless of P&L you pass.

If the Challenge fails, responsibility lies with the participant, not with the market, the rules, or the framework.

Document intent. This file defines the only acceptable formats for recording activity during the Challenge. The templates below are not optional at the ledger level and remain part of the evaluation framework. Purpose Section I Official Ledger Ledger Rules Ledger Fields Ledger Restrictions Section II Journal Journal Template Journal Restrictions Final Reminder

Purpose of this document

This document defines the only acceptable formats for recording activity during the Challenge.

The templates below are not optional. They are part of the evaluation framework.

The goal is to remove narrative bias, prevent hindsight justification, and preserve objective process data.

Mandatory

Section I Official Ledger

The ledger is a factual record, not a diary.

It answers one question only: What happened, measured objectively?

No interpretation is allowed. No explanation is allowed.

Ledger rules (non-negotiable)

  • Every executed trade must appear in the ledger.
  • No trade means no ledger entry.
  • Entries are made after the trade is closed.
  • Historical entries may never be edited.
  • Any deviation constitutes Ledger Contamination and fails the Challenge.

Ledger fields (exact structure)

Each row represents one trade.

Required columns:

  • Date
  • Instrument
  • Session Window (NY Core / NY Continuation)
  • Direction (Long / Short)
  • Result (RR)
  • Result (USD)
  • Trade Duration

No additional columns are permitted.

Example ledger entry

Date | Instrument | Session Window | Direction | RR | USD | Duration

2026-02-01 | US100 | NY Continuation | Long | +3.40 | +850.00 | 1h12m

Explicitly forbidden in the ledger

  • comments,
  • screenshots,
  • emotional notes,
  • strategy names,
  • explanations of decisions.

The ledger must remain emotion-free.

Optional

Section II Journal

The journal exists outside the ledger.

It is not used for evaluation. It is used for self-observation and calibration.

The journal must never be used to justify ledger results.

Journal rules and daily template

Journal rules

  • Journal entries are written after the session, not during.
  • No price levels or technical analysis.
  • No P&L celebration or frustration.

The journal answers one question only: How did I behave relative to the rules today?

Template (one page per day)

  • Date
  • Trades executed: 0 / 1 / 2 / 3
  • Session type: No-Trade Day / Single-Trade Day / Post-Loss Day / Post-Win Day
  • Rule adherence: Did I respect time windows? Yes / No
  • Rule adherence: Did I respect SL rules? Yes / No
  • Rule adherence: Did I avoid re-entries? Yes / No
  • Psychological observations: Where did I feel hesitation?
  • Psychological observations: Where did I feel pressure to act?
  • Process assessment: PASS / FAIL

Journal restrictions

The journal must not rewrite trade outcomes, reinterpret decisions, or contradict ledger facts.

If journal content conflicts with the ledger, the ledger is considered authoritative.

Separation

Final reminder

The ledger measures what you did. The journal helps you understand why it felt difficult.

Confusing these two destroys discipline.

Respecting this separation is part of passing the Challenge.

Document intent. This guide explains how to operate inside the Challenge on a day-to-day basis. It does not add new rules. It clarifies how the existing rules are applied in real conditions. If any conflict exists between this guide and the Official Challenge Document, the Official Challenge Document always takes precedence.

Purpose Core Principle Daily Operating Model 1. No-Trade Day 2. Single-Trade Day 3. Post-Loss Day 4. Post-Win Day Trading Windows Discipline Handling Missed Setups Emotional States End-of-Day Procedure Final Reminder

Purpose of this document

This guide explains how to operate inside the Challenge on a day-to-day basis.

It does not add new rules. It clarifies how the existing rules are applied in real conditions.

If there is any conflict between this guide and the Official Challenge Document, the Official Challenge Document always takes precedence.

Non-Negotiable

Core principle

You are not evaluated by outcomes. You are evaluated by behavior under uncertainty.

A day with no trades can be a perfect day. A profitable day with rule violations is a failed day.

Daily operating model

Each trading day falls into one of four categories. Your job is not to force a specific category.

Your job is to recognize which category the day belongs to and act accordingly.

1. No-Trade Day (Valid and Correct)

Definition: A session in which no A / A+ setup appears within the allowed trading windows.

Correct behavior:

  • No trades executed.
  • No anticipation entries.
  • No widening or bending of criteria.

Evaluation: PASS

Notes:

  • No-trade days are expected and normal.
  • Edge materializes only on a minority of sessions.
  • Forcing trades on these days is the primary cause of long-term failure.

2. Single-Trade Day (Standard)

Definition: One fully compliant trade executed within the allowed window.

Correct behavior:

  • One setup.
  • One execution.
  • No re-entries if the setup is missed.

Evaluation:

  • PASS if rules are respected.
  • FAIL if rules are modified mid-trade.

Notes:

  • The Challenge is designed around 0-1 trade per day.
  • Consistency is built here, not on high-activity days.

3. Post-Loss Day (Psychological Test)

Definition: A day where the first valid trade results in a loss.

Correct behavior:

  • Accept the loss as a correct outcome if rules were followed.
  • Immediately reset bias.
  • No revenge trading.

Evaluation:

  • PASS if no behavioral deviation occurs.
  • FAIL if loss leads to rule bending or impulsive trades.

Notes:

  • The Challenge is not testing your ability to avoid losses.
  • It is testing your ability to remain disciplined after them.

4. Post-Win Day (Hidden Risk)

Definition: A day where the first trade produces a strong positive result.

Correct behavior:

  • No pressure to trade again.
  • No increase in position size.
  • No change in rules due to confidence or excitement.

Evaluation:

  • PASS if no additional impulsive trades are taken.
  • FAIL if the win alters decision standards.

Notes:

  • Most rule violations occur after wins, not after losses.
  • Self-control after success is a core skill being tested.

Trading windows discipline

Trades are permitted only within the predefined windows:

  • NY Core Session
  • NY Continuation Session

Outside these windows, no trade is the default and correct decision.

Opening a position outside the allowed windows invalidates the Challenge regardless of outcome.

Handling missed setups

Missed setups are considered closed opportunities.

Correct response:

  • No re-entries.
  • No late executions.
  • No justification based on hindsight.

Re-entering after a missed setup is treated as a rule violation.

Emotional states and decision quality

You are expected to experience:

  • hesitation,
  • uncertainty,
  • discomfort near Stop Loss,
  • frustration during no-trade days.

These states are not problems. Acting on them is.

The correct action is always to return to the rules, not to emotions.

End-of-day procedure

At the end of each session:

  • Update the ledger with RR, USD, and duration only.
  • Do not add narrative explanations.
  • Do not reinterpret the day.

The ledger is a factual record, not a diary.

Restraint

Final reminder

This Challenge does not reward activity. It rewards restraint.

If you complete the Challenge by respecting the process regardless of P&L you pass.

If you fail, the market is not responsible. You are.
