Section 01

Behavioral Analysis Overview

Beyond structural compliance, PREPARE incorporates behavioral analysis through the Trader OS integration. This system evaluates not just whether you followed the rules, but how you followed them — detecting patterns that indicate control loss, emotional trading, or process degradation.

Behavioral metrics provide diagnostic feedback. They do not directly affect compliance status but offer insight into trading patterns that may predict future non-compliance or indicate areas requiring development.

Purpose: Structural compliance tells you IF you followed the process. Behavioral analysis tells you HOW you followed it — and whether your execution quality is sustainable.

Section 02

TradeScore System

Each trade submission receives a TradeScore from 0-100, calculated from four weighted components:

Compliance Component (40%)

Measures adherence to the six structural criteria. Full compliance scores 100%. Any violation drops this component to 0%.

Risk Component (25%)

Evaluates risk management quality. Factors include: risk percentage relative to 0.25% limit, stop loss placement logic, and position sizing accuracy.

Quality Component (20%)

Assesses execution precision. Factors include: data completeness, screenshot quality, timestamp accuracy, and documentation thoroughness.

Behavior Component (15%)

Analyzes trading patterns. Factors include: time between trades, same-day trade frequency, post-loss behavior, and session consistency.

Section 03

Session Zones

Your cumulative behavioral performance places you in one of three session zones. These zones provide real-time feedback on your trading state.

Zone Score Range Indication
GREEN 80-100 Optimal execution. Process discipline maintained. Continue current approach.
YELLOW 50-79 Caution zone. Minor deviations detected. Review recent patterns.
RED 0-49 Control loss indicated. Process breakdown likely. Consider pausing activity.

Note: Session zones are diagnostic, not punitive. A RED zone does not automatically trigger disqualification. However, participants consistently in RED zone typically accumulate non-compliant trades rapidly.

Section 04

Pattern Detection

The behavioral system monitors for specific patterns that indicate control loss or emotional trading:

Rapid Succession Trading

Multiple trades within a short time window, especially following a loss. This pattern often indicates revenge trading or emotional decision-making rather than systematic execution.

Post-Loss Escalation

Increased position sizing or trading frequency immediately after a losing trade. This pattern suggests emotional response to loss rather than adherence to a systematic approach.

Same-Day Overtrading

Excessive number of trades within a single session. High trade frequency often correlates with reduced decision quality and increased non-compliance rates.

Risk Boundary Testing

Consistently sizing positions at or near the 0.25% limit. While technically compliant, this pattern suggests the participant is pushing against constraints rather than operating comfortably within them.

Section 05

PREPARE Integration

Behavioral metrics integrate with PREPARE in the following ways:

  • Real-time feedback: TradeScore and session zone displayed after each submission
  • Pattern alerts: Warnings when behavioral patterns indicate elevated risk
  • History analysis: Cumulative behavioral trends visible in trade history
  • Qualification context: Behavioral summary included in qualification status

Using Behavioral Data

Behavioral metrics are most valuable as early warning indicators. A declining TradeScore trend or shift toward YELLOW/RED zone often precedes non-compliant submissions. Participants who monitor and respond to these signals can adjust their approach before accumulating violations.

Key Insight: The behavioral system reveals what structural compliance cannot: whether your process execution is stable and sustainable, or whether you are operating at the edge of control loss. Use this information proactively.
