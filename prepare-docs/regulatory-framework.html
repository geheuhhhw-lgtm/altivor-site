Section 01

Purpose & Authority

This Regulatory Framework establishes the governing principles, operational boundaries, and evaluation standards for the PREPARE qualification system. It serves as the authoritative reference for all procedural matters within the qualification process.

PREPARE exists as a mandatory procedural gate. Its purpose is to validate whether a participant can execute a declared trading process without deviation, prior to gaining access to advanced framework products.

Mandate: No participant may access the US100 Challenge or Framework Pack without first demonstrating procedural compliance through PREPARE qualification.

Section 02

Scope of Application

This framework applies to all participants who have purchased PREPARE access and are actively engaged in the qualification process. The scope includes:

  • All trade submissions within the PREPARE qualification system
  • All compliance evaluations and status determinations
  • All disqualification events and cooldown periods
  • All documentation requirements and submission standards

The framework does not govern trading activity outside the PREPARE system. Participants remain solely responsible for their own trading decisions, risk management, and financial outcomes.

Section 03

Definitions

The following terms carry specific meaning within this framework:

  • Compliant Trade: A trade submission that meets all structural requirements and passes compliance evaluation.
  • Non-Compliant Trade: A trade submission that fails one or more compliance criteria, regardless of financial outcome.
  • Qualification Threshold: The minimum number of compliant trades required to achieve QUALIFIED status (10 compliant trades).
  • Disqualification Limit: The maximum number of non-compliant trades before mandatory cooldown (20 non-compliant trades).
  • Cooldown Period: A mandatory 7-day suspension following disqualification, during which no submissions are accepted.
Section 04

Evaluation Standard

PREPARE operates on a binary evaluation model. Each trade submission is classified as either PASS (compliant) or FAIL (non-compliant). There is no partial compliance, no weighted scoring for individual submissions, and no consideration of financial outcome.

Core Principle: Financial outcome carries zero evaluative weight. A losing trade that follows all structural requirements is compliant. A profitable trade that violates any requirement is non-compliant.

The evaluation system measures procedural discipline, not trading skill. It validates whether the participant can follow a declared process consistently, regardless of market conditions or trade results.

Section 05

Participant Obligations

By engaging with the PREPARE qualification system, participants accept the following obligations:

  • Submit only genuine trades executed on a live or demo trading account
  • Provide accurate data that matches broker records exactly
  • Include required TradingView chart screenshots for each submission
  • Accept all compliance evaluations as final and non-negotiable
  • Respect cooldown periods without attempting circumvention
  • Maintain sole responsibility for all trading decisions and outcomes

Violation of these obligations may result in immediate disqualification, account suspension, or permanent access revocation at the sole discretion of ALTIVOR INSTITUTE.

Section 06

Enforcement & Finality

All determinations made under this framework are final. The evaluation system does not recognize appeals based on:

  • Good intentions or honest mistakes
  • Strong profit or favorable market conditions
  • Technical difficulties or platform issues
  • Emotional explanations or external circumstances

The framework exists to remove negotiation. The moment a participant requires flexibility beyond the stated rules, the framework has already performed its diagnostic function — revealing that the participant is not yet operating with the procedural discipline required for advanced framework access.

Finality Statement: PREPARE is designed to be strict. Disqualification is expected. The system functions correctly when it identifies participants who are not yet ready for advanced framework products.
